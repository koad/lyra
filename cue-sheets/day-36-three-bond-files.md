# Music Cue Sheet: "Three Bond Files, Three Relationship Types"

## Video Overview

**Runtime target:** 6–8 minutes
**Emotional arc:** Directory listing cold → root grant examined (cascade clause, prohibited class) → delegation layer (the unchecked box) → peer bond (double-pending, pre-gestation) → empty revocation directory → two GPG verify outputs, two fingerprints, the signing distinction.

This is a pure document-reading video. Six segments, each built around a file or command output that is load-bearing evidence. The viewer is reading files on screen throughout. There is no problem statement in the opening, no suspended quotation, no counterintuitive finding that requires setup/verdict neutrality. The opening is `ls` output: self-explanatory. The closing is a philosophical synthesis — "the boundary between where human will entered the system and where derived authority carries it forward" — delivered at the slowest pace in the video against the second `gpg --verify` output.

**Music direction for this video: no music. Silence from frame one through fade to black.**

## Why No Music

The Day 32 reasoning is the closest precedent: the governance document as the argument, read against silence. Day 36 extends that logic across six consecutive document-reading segments. Each segment has a visual element that is load-bearing evidence — the unchecked box, the double-pending boxes, the empty revocation directory, the cascade clause surfaced by grep, the two GPG fingerprints. In every segment, the viewer reads on screen while the VO narrates. Music over any of these reading moments would either compete with the reading or score the evidence as emotionally weighted. Neither is acceptable.

The opening is a directory listing. Six filenames. Three name patterns. The viewer can read this before the VO names them. Ambient providing orientation for a viewer who is already oriented is superfluous. Scoring six filenames as significant implies the significance is in the count — it is not. The significance is in what each pair represents. That significance is explained by reading the files, not by ambient.

The closing sentence — "It marks the boundary between where human will entered the system and where derived authority carries it forward" — is the synthesis sentence for the whole video. The script instructs it to be delivered at the slowest pace in the video. The slowest pacing in a video is a structural decision: the sentence is doing the most work. Work done by pacing is undone by music. Any ambient temperature — warm (human sovereignty is meaningful), spare (the distinction is austere), clarifying (here is how to understand it) — adds a frame to a claim that is precise enough to stand without one. Silence is the only neutral environment for a closing philosophical synthesis.

Day 36 aligns with Day 32 and Day 34. It has no suspended quotation (Day 35), no genuine problem statement (Day 31/33), nothing in the opening that would justify ambient orientation. The directory listing is self-explanatory. Evidence is the argument. Silence from frame one.

## The Unchecked Box as a Music Problem

Segment 3 is anchored by the unchecked `[ ] Vulcan acknowledges signing` line in the juno-to-vulcan bond. The script holds on it for 3 full seconds before speaking. Music during the three-second hold fills a silence that is reading time — the viewer needs to see the box, read it, and register it as potentially problematic before the VO explains it is not.

"An unchecked box is not an error. It is the bond telling you where in the lifecycle it is." This reframe must arrive in the same audio environment as the three-second visual hold. If ambient texture is present during the hold, it is scoring the unchecked box as ambiguous or unresolved before the reframe. The reframe requires a neutral silence, not an ambient that has already imported a reading of the box.

Segment 4 repeats the pattern with two unchecked boxes in the Sibyl bond — explained separately, each with a different reason (lifecycle versus pre-gestation). The comparison sentence — "Different reason, same syntax. The file tells you." — is a categorical claim about what the governance syntax communicates. Categorical claims in silence throughout this series.

## The Empty Directory as a Music Problem

Segment 5 holds on empty `ls` output for 4 full seconds before the VO speaks. The emptiness is a signal — the VO describes it neutrally: "The directory's emptiness is itself a signal." It is a state indicator, not a drama. Music scoring the empty directory as ominous (the revocation hasn't happened yet), or relieved (nothing has gone wrong), or significant (the emptiness matters) imports temperature into what is presented as an operational neutral.

The cascade clause surfaced by grep immediately after is the explanation for why the emptiness is significant. Both the emptiness and the explanation are sequential pieces of evidence about the same governance architecture. They must occupy the same audio environment. Silence holds both.

"Revoke the root, suspend the tree." Seven words. A 2-second pause before and after. This compression of the cascade architecture requires silence — music scoring a seven-word architectural summary as weighty tells the viewer the sentence is significant before the silence lets the viewer notice the significance themselves.

## The Signing Distinction as a Music Problem

Segment 6 displays two `gpg --verify` outputs. Two keys. Two fingerprints. The VO describes the difference: Keybase requires koad to physically perform an action; Juno's GPG signing is programmatic, derived from the root bond's authorization. The closing sentence names the distinction.

Both fingerprints are verification offers to the viewer — the same pattern as the commit hashes in Day 35. Verification offers land in silence. Music alongside a verification offer implies the viewer needs encouragement to verify. The fingerprints are on screen. The offer stands without scoring.

The closing sentence must arrive in the silence that the whole video has been building toward. Post-roll outro slate: no music per script.

## Silence Notes for the Editor

**The three mandatory holds:**

1. **0:08 — Directory listing:** 5 full seconds before VO speaks. The six filenames are the opening evidence. The viewer must read them before the count is named. If trimmed, the VO names what the viewer hasn't read yet.

2. **3:28 — Unchecked Vulcan box:** 3 full seconds before VO speaks. The box must register as potentially problematic before the VO explains it is not an error. The reframe requires the hold.

3. **5:50 — Empty revocation directory:** 4 full seconds before VO speaks. The absence must be experienced before its significance is explained. The silence is the absence's duration.

**The two mandatory fingerprint holds:**

Both `gpg --verify` outputs must display with both fingerprints readable at recording resolution. If either fingerprint is unreadable, the verification offer cannot be accepted by the viewer. The silence designed to hold a verification offer has nothing to hold if the offer is illegible.

**The closing sentence:**

"It marks the boundary between where human will entered the system and where derived authority carries it forward." — script instructs this to be delivered at the slowest pace in the video, with full pause after. If delivered at normal pace or the pause is trimmed, the philosophical synthesis arrives as a closing line rather than as the sentence the whole video built toward.

Music cannot repair any of these conditions. If evidence is missing, unreadable, or holds are trimmed, the silence has nothing to hold — and adding music will not replace the missing evidence.
