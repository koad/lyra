## Opening Music Brief

**What the first 5 seconds should feel like:**

Day 7. Something ran for a week and came out the other side. The viewer doesn't know that yet — they're looking at an empty terminal. The music should feel like a room that has been occupied for a long time. Not empty. Not neutral. There's weight to the silence that the music carries just underneath. A single low drone or sustained pad, nearly subliminal. Tempo-free. No rhythm. The goal is to set a register — not excitement, not solemnity, just: this matters, pay attention.

---

## Key Transition Points

**1. 4:40 — The first commit lines appear in `git log`**
This is the most important musical moment in the video. The git log is described in both the script and shot list as "the emotional core — do not cut." The music needs to acknowledge that. When the first commit line appears, the melodic motif should enter — slightly fuller than it has been anywhere else in the video. The accumulation should feel earned because it is earned: 7 days of real work is appearing line by line. Timing: motif enters on the first commit line, not on the command being typed. Let the viewer arrive at the log before the music responds to it.

**2. 5:30 — Musical peak during log scroll**
The only moment in the video where the music reaches full expression. "Full" here is relative — this is not a film score. Full means: warm pad texture, motif present, maybe a second harmonic layer. Still no drums. Still no percussion. The feeling is late-night satisfaction, not cinematic victory. It should feel like someone who has been working quietly for a long time looking at what they built. The peak holds for 20–30 seconds, then begins its descent.

**3. 7:00 — `echo "The entity's state is files on disk."` — music exits**
The script says "hold 3–4 seconds." That line is the thesis close. Music should be completely absent by the time this command runs. The correct exit point is anywhere in the 6:30–6:50 range — the exact frame where the music ends is a post-production judgment call, but it must be gone before this echo runs. The silence after the line prints is intentional and structural. Do not refill it.

---

## Closing Brief

**How the video should feel in its final 10 seconds:**

The viewer has watched the git log fill a screen. They've heard "files on disk don't age." They're now watching a git clone start — the operation that the whole video is about, happening live, one more time. The music is gone. What remains is the terminal output and the implicit invitation: you can do this. The video ends before the clone completes. That's deliberate — the story doesn't end here, and the music shouldn't pretend it does. Silence, or ambient room tone, under the first clone output lines. Then cut.

No music on the outro slate. No music over the GitHub URL. The viewer should leave with the terminal in their head, not a sound.

## Source Suggestions

1. **Deep ambient drone, single tone, 30–60 second sustain** — for the opening and closing. No movement. Just presence. Good candidates: synth pad in low C or D, slow LFO modulation, no attack. Should feel like occupied silence, not empty silence.

2. **Minimalist lo-fi ambient, 70–80 BPM, no percussion** — for the git log sequence (4:30–6:00). The motif here should be warm — a muted piano, kalimba, or soft plucked string. Single notes or minimal two-note phrases. Not a melody that goes somewhere dramatic — a melody that acknowledges something quietly. This track is the emotional spine of the video.

3. **Slow evolving harmonic pad, C major or A minor feel** — for the argument section (1:00–2:30). Something that feels like a thesis being constructed: deliberate, unhurried, with forward motion. Should pair well with VO without distracting from it. No drums.

4. **Sparse electronic texture, no melody, near-subliminal** — for the technical hook section (2:30–4:20). The function of this track is to prevent the technical section from feeling dead while not pulling attention away from the code on screen. Sub-bass texture or very slow filter movement. As low in the mix as possible.

5. **Single-note piano decay or ambient close** — for the final 60 seconds (6:30 onward). If any music at all. A single note that lasts 15–20 seconds and then is gone. No resolution. No chord. Just the note and then silence. The video earns this restraint.
